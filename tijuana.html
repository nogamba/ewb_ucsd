Tijuana

Agua Pour Favor International Project in Tijuana, Mexico

About our project

Our project in Tijuana, Mexico is non-EWB USA affiliated and was established by the EWB-UCSD student group. This project works to provide a cheaper, more consistent source of clean water to an orphanage in the city of Tijuana. The orphanage, Casa de Hogar Maria Inmaculada, currently buys their water from a local water treatment plant, spending a few hundred dollars monthly on 5000 L of water, which must be rationed amongst more than 50 people per month.

Local and free sources of water, such as tap water, cannot be consumed due to bacterial contamination, high lead concentrations from the piping, and high pH. The EWB-UCSD Tijuana team is currently researching methods to rid of lead and bacteria from water and decrease the pH. Additionally, the team is prototyping a small scale filtration system on campus prior to installation.
